📖 What is the Stock Average Calculator?

The Stock Average Calculator is a valuable tool for investors looking to manage their portfolio effectively. It calculates your weighted average cost per share across multiple purchase lots, giving you a clear picture of your actual cost basis — and helping you make smarter decisions about when and how much to buy.

💡 Why Use It?

As an investor, you may encounter situations where a stock's price moves contrary to your expectations. For example, say you bought Reliance stocks anticipating an upward trend — but the market moves downward. If your conviction in the stock remains strong, you can strategically buy more shares at the lower price to bring down your average cost per share. This approach is known as averaging down, and this calculator makes it precise and effortless.

🪜 How to Use

1
Input Purchase Prices

Enter the price per share and number of shares for each time you bought the stock. Add as many purchase lots as needed.

2
Calculate Average Price

Hit Calculate Average — the tool computes the weighted average price across all your purchase lots instantly.

3
Make an Informed Decision

Compare the calculated average to the current market price. If the market is below your average, consider if buying more makes sense for your strategy.

4
Reduce Your Average Price

By buying additional shares at a lower price, your new weighted average drops — reducing the price target needed to break even or turn a profit.

5
Review the Updated Output

The calculator shows your new weighted average, total units held, and total capital invested — giving you a complete picture of your position.

⚠️

A word of caution: Averaging down can be a powerful strategy, but it also increases exposure to a single stock. Always exercise caution, stay well-informed, and consider market conditions and your own risk tolerance before making additional purchases.
